Human-Code
Manifesto

  • We distinguish between AI as a tool and AI as a replacement. One augments craft; the other erases it.
  • We reject hype-driven development. Features exist to solve real user problems, not to satisfy investor narratives.
  • We demand that AI productivity gains translate to better working conditions — not just higher output quotas.
  • We refuse to be called "Prompt Engineers." We are software engineers. Our expertise took years, not a ChatGPT subscription.
  • We will name and resist the political entanglements of AI infrastructure — including its concentration of power.
  • We stand for a future where technology serves humanity, with a 3-day work week as proof that it can.
  • We are the Programmers Guild. We build. We resist. We code.
